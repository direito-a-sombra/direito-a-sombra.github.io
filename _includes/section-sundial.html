Sundial

I've always been fascinated by sunlight shadow angles, and this project allowed me to investigate how solar incidence can draw a city.

What if in urban planning, we consider equal amounts of sun and shadows? Based on that question, we developed a digital sundial with a lot of math that flattens, projects and calculates the angles of the solar incidence in Fortaleza (depending on time and month) into a generic pole (adjustable height), and the shadow created, with a fixed viewpoint location facing south. The main goal was, after these calculations, to create a square divided into equal parts of sun and shadow, a perfect equilibrium, a kind of yin-yang.

The Spatial Shadow Calendar series, a series of speculative data-paintings, where light and dark occupies 50% of the surface area.
